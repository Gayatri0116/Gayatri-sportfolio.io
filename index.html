Research Assistant

Virginia Commonwealth University

May 2023 - August 2023

Conducted research on using Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) for synthetic data generation to enhance anomaly detection in CAN bus systems for autonomous vehicles.

Key contributions:

  • Developed and fine-tuned a custom GAN architecture with LeakyReLU, batch normalization, and optimized hyperparameters for realistic data synthesis.
  • Performed exploratory data analysis on CAN bus communication logs and implemented preprocessing including MinMax scaling and one-hot encoding.
  • Addressed data imbalance and feature redundancy to improve anomaly detection model training.
  • Achieved adversarial equilibrium with discriminator accuracy converging near 50%, indicating high-quality synthetic data generation.
  • Demonstrated competitive results compared to existing GAN approaches through stable loss metrics and statistical similarity in generated data.
  • Proposed implications for cybersecurity applications and generalization to other domains with limited anomaly data.

Research

Exploring the intersection of AI, ML, and complex tasks

Generative Adversarial Networks for Anomaly Detection in Autonomous Vehicles

Led research on applying GANs to enhance anomaly detection within CAN bus systems in autonomous vehicles, addressing the challenges of data scarcity and real-time threat identification.

Research highlights:

  • Designed a custom GAN architecture with LeakyReLU activations and batch normalization for stable synthetic anomaly generation.
  • Performed extensive data preprocessing including normalization and one-hot encoding to optimize model input quality.
  • Generated high-fidelity synthetic attack data that improved downstream detection models.
  • Achieved adversarial balance with discriminator accuracy ~50%, validating the realism of generated data.
  • Demonstrated potential to reduce false positives and improve safety-critical communication in vehicle systems.

The research findings contributed to improved cybersecurity protocols for autonomous vehicle communication systems and were presented at the International Conference on Machine Learning Applications (ICMLA).
